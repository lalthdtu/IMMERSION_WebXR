About IMMERSION

Decision-making permeates every aspect of our lives, from daily commutes to major life choices. Understanding the 'why' behind our decisions is crucial for addressing global challenges like climate change and public health. Yet, traditional methods often fail to capture the complexity of human thought processes, focusing only on the final outcome.

IMMERSION aims to change that. This project develops new methods to combine traditional choice data with rich 'process data'—such as eye-tracking and neurophysiological measurements—collected in highly realistic, immersive Extended Reality (XR) environments. By creating a more complete picture of decision-making, IMMERSION will equip researchers with a powerful new toolkit to explain and predict human behaviour with unprecedented accuracy.

The insights gained will have far-reaching impacts, particularly for the human-centric design of future transport systems, and will create a paradigm shift in behavioural research across many fields.

Core Objectives

O1

Make Models Applicable

Make advanced behavioural models applicable to real-world problems.

O2

Leverage Process Data

Formulate new models that leverage process data to better explain and predict decisions.

O3

Develop New Inference Procedures

Devise new computationally efficient statistical inference procedures for large-scale applications.

O4

Advance Data Collection

Develop and test new approaches for collecting choice and process data at scale using immersive experiments.

O5

Apply to Real-World Problems

Apply these methods to provide new insights into human decision-making and quantify the importance of process data.

How It Works: Research Streams

RS1: Data Collection

Developing and deploying crowdsourced immersive XR experiments to gather rich behavioural data, including biomarkers, neuroimaging (EEG), and eye-tracking.

RS2: Modelling

Creating new modelling approaches that integrate choice data with various types of process data under a unified framework to improve explanatory and predictive power.

RS3: Statistical Inference

Deriving new, computationally efficient statistical methods to make these advanced models fast, scalable, and accessible for empirical work.

RS4: Applications

Applying these innovations to practical case studies, including urban transport infrastructure, pedestrian-AV interactions, and wayfinding, to generate new insights.

Case Studies & Applications

IMMERSION will put its methodological innovations into practice through extensive empirical work with implications for the human-centric design of future transport systems. The case studies will derive profound new behavioural insights in the contexts of:

Transportation Infrastructure

Studying how people make decisions about new transportation infrastructure.

Pedestrian-AV Interactions

Investigating the dynamics between pedestrians and autonomous vehicles.

Pedestrian Wayfinding

Analyzing how people navigate through different environments.
