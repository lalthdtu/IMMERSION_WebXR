About IMMERSION

Decision-making is a core element of human activity, and the decisions we make can have significant consequences for ourselves, others, and the world we live in. Thus, the ability to explain and predict decision-making is vital for improving individual and societal outcomes, especially when addressing global challenges like climate change, sustainable energy, ageing populations, and public health. Mathematical models of decision-making can help us to understand and predict human behaviour.

Despite significant progress, several scientific challenges persist in the applicability of advanced behavioural models to real-world problems. Many models focus on predicting decision outcomes rather than explaining the decision process itself. Information contained in neurophysiological process data that emerge during decision-making are often ignored, and methods for collecting behavioural data fail to create realistic impressions of scenarios representing the future.

IMMERSION aims to advance the study of human decision-making by developing new innovative methods for combining choice and process data. This includes new models for integrating choice and process data, new statistical inference procedures tailored to such models, and new methods for collecting rich behavioural data in immersive experiments. The proposed research will create a paradigm shift in behavioural research with impacts on many application domains. IMMERSION will equip researchers with a new powerful toolkit for extracting deep behavioural insights from rich data using advanced models.

Core Objectives

O1

Make advanced behavioural models applicable to real-world problems.

Make advanced behavioural models applicable to real-world problems.

O2

Leverage Process Data

Formulate new behavioural models that leverage the information contained in various types of process data to better explain and predict decision-making.

O3

Devise new computationally efficient statistical inference procedures

Devise new computationally efficient statistical inference procedures to facilitate the integration of choice and process data in large-scale applications.

O4

Advance Data Collection

Develop and test new approaches for collecting choice and process data at scale using immersive experiments.

O5

Apply to Real-World Problems

Apply the newly developed methods to real-world problems to provide new insights into human decision-making and quantify the absolute and relative importance of different types of process data.

How It Works: Research Streams

RS1: Data Collection

Tapping into the full potential of immersive XR experiments for gathering choice and process data, including establishing an XR lab and formulating procedures for crowdsourcing experiments to the web.

RS2: Modelling

Formulating and testing new modelling approaches for integrating choice data and various types of process data under a unified framework.

RS3: Statistical Inference

Deriving, implementing, and evaluating new statistical inference procedures tailored to advanced behavioural models that integrate choice and process data.

RS4: Applications

Putting methodological innovations to practical use in three case studies to provide new insights into human decision-making with implications for the human-centric design of transport systems.

Case Studies & Applications

The proposed research includes substantial empirical work applying IMMERSION's methodological innovations to real-world problems with implications for the human-centric design of future transport systems. This work includes case studies to explain and predict human decision-making in the contexts of:

Transportation Infrastructure

Studying human decision-making in the context of transportation infrastructure development.

Pedestrian-AV Interactions

Investigating decision-making related to pedestrian-autonomous vehicle interactions.

Pedestrian Wayfinding

Analyzing human decision-making in the context of pedestrian wayfinding.
